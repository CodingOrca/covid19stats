Symbols

For every day since the beginning of case reporting, we define the following indicators:
Δ - new cases reported for that day
Σ - total cases reported until and including on that day
R - cases known as recovered on that day
A - cases known to be on that day
C - cases known as critical on that day (subset of A)
Θ - total deaths reported until and including that day
R0 - reproduction number, reverse calculated from the data of the last 5 days. This is the expected average number of persons which will be infected by one infected person
Note: We calculate an "assumed recovered" indicator, based on the average infection period, and show it in the graphics with another color. When we calculate the reproduction rates and number, we also consider these calculated cases as recovered.

Math

We are using a very simple but illustrative model to simulate what could happen in the future, based on the logistic equation.

Reproduction rate and number

Let us assume that an infected person, on average, is encountering every day a number ρ of other persons. For each person he encounters, there is a probability φ of infecting him (for instance if they shake hands, or the infected person is sneezing while they meet etc.).

Definition:The reproduction rate r0 is the number of new infections caused directly by an infected person during one day, if all other persons around him are susceptible: r0= ρ × φ

If on day n there are An (A from Active) contagious persons, and assuming all of them meet only susceptible persons (meaning: persons which can be infected - i.e. they are not yet infected, nor immune) - then during one day, the new number of infected persons is:

Δn+1 = r0 × An

Notes:
1. If r0 ≤ 1, the disease cannot spread and disappears.
2. The reproduction rate is influenced by the social habits and behavior of the people (by ρ: how many other people are met; by φ: do they shake hands? Do they embrace each other? Do they wash their hands on a regular base?).
3. It is also influenced by the disease itself (by ρ: how it is transmitted?).
4. It is NOT influenced by the status of the other persons - i.e. if they are already immune or not.

Definition: The Infection period τ is the duration an infected person remains contagious and can infect others.

Definition: The reproduction number R0 is the number of persons directly infected by an infected person during his infection period.

R0 = r0 × τ

Both r0 and R0 are defined under ideal conditions: it is assumed that all other persons are susceptible. This is the case in the beginning of an epidemy, when the number of active and immune persons is negligible. But after some time, our imaginary infected person will not only encounter susceptible persons, but also active or immune persons. If the population of the country is P, the number of current infected persons is A (active), R the recovered persons, and D the persons who died, then the number of susceptible persons is S = P - A - R - D, and the probability for our imaginary infected person to encounter a susceptible person is s = S / P. The probability s will decrease from 1 towards 0 during an epidemy (we will see later that often it will not reach 0).
The newly infected persons on day n can be written as:

Δn+1 = r0 * An * sn
sn = (P - An - Rn -Dn) / P

The number of recovered persons on day n can be written as

Rn = Rn-1 + Δn-τ    for n ≥ τ
and
Rn = Rn-1    for 0 < n < τ
And the number of infected persons on day n is:

An = An-1 + Δn - Δn-τ    for n ≥ τ
and
An = An-1 + Δn    for 0 < n < τ

For now we assume there are no deaths:

Dn = 0    for all n

Our result is a slightly more complex form of the logistic map. Below we plot the series for the 2 indicators: An and Rn. We quickly observe that the values of R and S have an asymptotic behavior so that not the whole population will be infected. A - the number of simultaneously ill persons - is growing exponential in the beginning, reaches a maximum and the falls down exponentially back to 0. The amplitude of this maximum, the point in time when is reached and the final values of R and S depends on the two unknown parameters of our series: τ and r0.

Simulation

All measures taken by the government of your country try to influence the basic reproduction rate r0 in such a way that the health system is not getting overloaded. In particular, the critical cases C defined above shall not exceed the ICUs (Intensive Care Units) available in your country. At the same time, the cases needing hospitalization shall not exceed the number of available beds in the hospitals.
From data analysed up to now, it seems that about 4.5% of all cases needs hospitalization, and about 25% of them will need intensive care. One of the sources of information is the Modellierung von Beispielszenarien der SARS-CoV-2-Epidemie 2020 in Deutschland.
